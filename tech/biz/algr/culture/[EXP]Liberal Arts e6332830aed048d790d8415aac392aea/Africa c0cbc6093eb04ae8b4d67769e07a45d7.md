2. العراق (Al-'Irāq) - Iraq
3. الأردن (Al-'Urdun) - Jordan
4. الكويت (Al-Kuwayt) - Kuwait
5. لبنان (Lubnān) - Lebanon
6. عُمان ('Umān) - Oman
7. فلسطين (Filasṭīn) - Palestine
8. قطر (Qaṭar) - Qatar
9. المملكة العربية السعودية (Al-Mamlakah al-'Arabīyah as-Su'ūdīyah) - Saudi Arabia
10. السودان (As-Sūdān) - Sudan
11. سوريا (Sūriyā) - Syria
12. الإمارات العربية المتحدة (Al-'Imārāt al-'Arabīyah al-Muttaḥidah) - United Arab Emirates
13. اليمن (Al-Yaman) - Yemen
14. جزر القمر (Juzur al-Qamar) - Comoros
15. Mauritania: Named after the ancient Berber kingdom of Mauretania.
16. Morocco: From "Marrakesh," a former capital city.
17. Tunisia: Named after its capital Tunis.
18. Libya: From the name of a Berber tribe called the Libu.
19. Egypt: From the Greek "Aigyptos," possibly derived from "hwt-ka-ptah" meaning "temple of the soul of Ptah".
20. 
21. Algeria: Derived from the Arabic "Al-Jazair," meaning "the islands," referring to the small islands off the coast of Algiers
22. Angola: Named after the Ngola, a title for kings of the Ndongo people.
23. 3. Benin: Named after the Bight of Benin, a bay in the Gulf of Guinea.
24. 4. Botswana: Means "land of the Tswana," referring to the country's dominant ethnic group.
25. 5. Burkina Faso: Means "land of incorruptible people" in Mossi and Dioula languages.
26. 6. Burundi: Derived from the Kirundi word for "land of the Rundi people".
27. 7. Cameroon: From the Portuguese "Rio dos Camarões" (River of Prawns), referring to the Wouri River.
28. 8. Cape Verde: Named after Cap-Vert (Green Cape) in Senegal.
29. 9. Central African Republic: Refers to its geographical location in central Africa.
30. 10. Chad: Named after Lake Chad, which may come from the local word "tsade" meaning "lake".
31. 11. Comoros: From the Arabic "qamar" meaning "moon".
32. 12. Congo: Named after the Kongo people.
33. 13. Democratic Republic of the Congo: Also named after the Kongo people.
34. 14. Djibouti: Possibly from the Afar word "gabouti," a type of doormat made from palm fibers.
35. 16. Equatorial Guinea: Refers to its location near the equator and its former colonizer, Spain.
36. 17. Eritrea: From the ancient Greek name for the Red Sea, "Erythra Thalassa".
37. 18. Ethiopia: From the Greek "Aithiopia," meaning "land of burnt faces".
38. 19. Gabon: Possibly from the Portuguese "gabão," a coat with sleeve and hood.
39. 20. Gambia: Named after the Gambia River, possibly from the Mandinka word "Kambra/Kambaa" meaning river.
40. 21. Ghana: Named after the medieval Ghana Empire.
41. 22. Guinea: Possibly from the Berber word "aguinaw" meaning "black".
42. 23. Guinea-Bissau: Combines Guinea with Bissau, its capital city.
43. 24. Ivory Coast (Côte d'Ivoire): Named for its ivory trade during colonial times.
44. 25. Kenya: Possibly named after Mount Kenya, from the Kikuyu word "kirinyaga" meaning "God's resting place".
45. 26. Lesotho: Means "land of the Sotho people".
46. 27. Liberia: From the Latin "liber" meaning "free," as it was founded by freed American slaves.
47. 29. Madagascar: Possibly from the Malagasy word "Madagasikara".
48. 30. Malawi: Means "flaming waters," referring to the reflection of the sun on Lake Malawi.
49. 31. Mali: Named after the medieval Mali Empire.
50. 33. Mauritius: Named after Dutch Prince Maurice van Nassau.
51. 35. Mozambique: Named after Mussa Bin Bique, an Arab sultan.
52. 36. Namibia: From the Namib Desert, meaning "vast place" in the Nama language.
53. 37. Niger: Named after the Niger River.
54. 38. Nigeria: Also named after the Niger River.
55. 39. Rwanda: Means "domain" in Kinyarwanda.
56. 40. São Tomé and Príncipe: Named after Saint Thomas and Prince's Island.
57. 41. Senegal: Possibly from the Wolof phrase "sunuu gaal" meaning "our boat".
58. 42. Seychelles: Named after Jean Moreau de Séchelles, a French minister of finance.
59. 43. Sierra Leone: From the Portuguese "Serra Leoa" meaning "lion mountains".
60. 44. Somalia: Possibly from the Somali "soo maal" meaning "go and milk".
61. 45. South Africa: Refers to its geographical location.
62. 46. South Sudan: Refers to its location in the southern part of Sudan.
63. 47. Sudan: From the Arabic "bilad as-sudan" meaning "land of the blacks".
64. 48. Swaziland (Eswatini): Means "land of the Swazi people".
65. 49. Tanzania: Combination of Tanganyika and Zanzibar.
66. 50. Togo: From the Ewe words "to" (water) and "go" (shore).
67. 52. Uganda: From the Buganda kingdom, meaning "land of the Ganda".
68. 53. Zambia: Named after the Zambezi River.
69. 54. Zimbabwe: From "dzimba dzemabwe" in Shona, meaning "houses of stone".
